Connect Four AI

This was part of the implementation of the Minimax algorithm for a computer player for Connect Four. It was coded in Racket, and was done as a lab assignment for the WPI course. "Accelerated Introduction to Program Design."

There are two evaluation functions: simple and complex. The simple checks for four in a rows on the board, while the complex looks for fours, threes, and twos in a row and values them accordingly. I approached this problem by drawing a row of checkers on a piece of paper. I walked myself through a potential approach, and I then translated it to code.

The computer-modes function is what implements the Minimax search. I also approached this by walking myself through the process step by step, and then translated that into code.
